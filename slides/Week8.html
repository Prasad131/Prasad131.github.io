Assumptions

  • Steady, laminar, 2D flow
  • Viscous dissipation term is neglected
  • Boundary layer approximation is valid (δT/H≪1\delta_T/H \ll 1)
  • Temperature difference between plate and flud is small enough to assume constant properties of the fluid
  • Flow is incompressible - however, density will vary with location. The assumption is valid because the change in density is due to changes in temperature field

Boussinesq Approximation

Treat density as constant in the continuity equation and the inertia terms of the momentum equation, but allow it to change with temperature in the gravity term

Thus, continuity equation becomes

∂u∂x+∂v∂y=0 \frac{ \partial u }{ \partial x } + \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial y } =0

X-momentum equation is neglected under boundary layer approximation, hence y-momentum equation becomes

u∂v∂x+v∂v∂y=ν∂2v∂x2+βg(T−T∞) u \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial x } +v \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial y } = \nu \frac{ \partial^{2} v }{ \partial x^{2} }+ \beta g(T-T_{\infty}) where β=−1ρ∂ρ∂T|P\beta=-\frac{1}{\rho}\left.\frac{ \partial \rho }{ \partial T }\right|_{P} is the volumetric expansion coefficient of the fluid

Energy equation becomes

u∂T∂x+v∂T∂y=α∂2T∂x2 u \frac{ \partial T }{ \partial x } +v \frac{ \partial T }{ \partial y } = \alpha \frac{ \partial^{2} T }{ \partial x^{2} }

Scale Analysis

x∼δTu∼αδTy∼Hv∼αHδT2 \begin{align} x & \sim & \delta_{T} & u & \sim & \frac{\alpha}{\delta_{T}} \\ y & \sim & H & v & \sim & \frac{\alpha H}{\delta_{T}^{2}} \end{align}

non-dimensional numbers governing the flow physics are

Prandlt number, Pr=ναRayleigh number, RaH=gβΔTH3αν \begin{align} \text{Prandlt number, } Pr & = \frac{\nu}{\alpha} \\ \text{Rayleigh number, } Ra_{H} & = \frac{g\beta \Delta TH^{3}}{\alpha \nu} \end{align}

Scaling analysis for the momentum equations gives us,

u∂v∂x+v∂v∂y=ν∂2v∂2+gβ(T−T∞) u \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial x } + v \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial y } = \nu \frac{ \partial^{2} v }{ \partial^{2} } + g\beta(T-T_{\infty})

u∂v∂x,v∂v∂y∼(HδT)4RaH−1Pr−1ν∂2v∂x2∼(HδT)4RaH−1gβ(T−T∞)∼1 \begin{align} u \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial x }, v \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial y } & \sim \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1}Pr^{-1} \\ \nu \frac{ \partial^{2} v }{ \partial x^{2} } & \sim \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1} \\ g\beta (T-T_{\infty}) & \sim 1 \end{align}

Nusselt number for Pr≫1Pr\gg 1

u∂v∂x,v∂v∂y∼(HδT)4RaH−1Pr−1ν∂2v∂x2∼(HδT)4RaH−1gβ(T−T∞)∼1 \begin{align} u \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial x }, v \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial y } & \sim \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1}Pr^{-1} \\ \nu \frac{ \partial^{2} v }{ \partial x^{2} } & \sim \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1} \\ g\beta (T-T_{\infty}) & \sim 1 \end{align}

If Pr≫1Pr\gg 1, then the inertial terms would be negligible or viscous and buoyancy forces balance each other

⟹(HδT)4RaH−1∼1 \implies \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1} \sim 1

which gives us an estimate of the boundary layer thickness as δTH∼RaH−1/4\frac{\delta_{T}}{H} \sim Ra_{H}^{-1/4}

Heat transfer coefficient is given by,

h=−κ∂T∂x|x=0Tw−T∞h∼κδT \begin{align} h & = \frac{-\kappa \left. \frac{ \partial T }{ \partial x } \right|_{x=0}}{T_{w}-T_{\infty}} \\ h & \sim \frac{\kappa}{\delta_{T}} \end{align}

If we define Nusselt number, NuH=hHκNu_{H}=\frac{hH}{\kappa}

⟹Nu∼HδT∼RaH1/4 \implies Nu \sim \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \sim Ra_{H}^{1/4}

Nusselt number for Pr≪1Pr\ll 1

u∂v∂x,v∂v∂y∼(HδT)4RaH−1Pr−1ν∂2v∂x2∼(HδT)4RaH−1gβ(T−T∞)∼1 \begin{align} u \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial x }, v \frac{ \partial v }{ \partial y } & \sim \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1}Pr^{-1} \\ \nu \frac{ \partial^{2} v }{ \partial x^{2} } & \sim \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1} \\ g\beta (T-T_{\infty}) & \sim 1 \end{align}

If Pr≪1Pr\ll 1, then the viscous terms would be negligible or inertial and buoyance forces balance each other

⟹(HδT)4RaH−1Pr−1∼1 \implies \left( \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \right)^{4}Ra_{H}^{-1}Pr^{-1} \sim 1

which gives us an estimate of the boundary layer thickness as δTH∼RaH−1/4Pr−1/4\frac{\delta_{T}}{H} \sim Ra_{H}^{-1/4}Pr^{-1/4}

Heat transfer coefficient is given by,

h=−κ∂T∂x|x=0Tw−T∞h∼κδT \begin{align} h & = \frac{-\kappa \left. \frac{ \partial T }{ \partial x } \right|_{x=0}}{T_{w}-T_{\infty}} \\ h & \sim \frac{\kappa}{\delta_{T}} \end{align}

If we define Nusselt number, NuH=hHκNu_{H}=\frac{hH}{\kappa}

⟹Nu∼HδT∼RaH1/4Pr1/4∼BoH1/4 \implies Nu \sim \frac{H}{\delta_{T}} \sim Ra_{H}^{1/4}Pr^{1/4} \sim Bo_{H}^{1/4}

where Boussinesq number, BoH=RaHPrBo_{H} = Ra_{H}Pr

Nusselt number for Pr≪1Pr\ll 1 and close to wall

Very close to the wall, inertial forces are small, hence viscous and buoyancy forces balance each other, say δν\delta_{\nu} is the thickness of this thin region

⟹ν∂2v∂x2∼gβ(T−T∞) \implies \nu \frac{ \partial^{2}v }{ \partial x^{2} } \sim g\beta(T-T_{\infty})

we obtain,

δνH∼GrH−1/4 \frac{\delta_{\nu}}{H} \sim Gr_{H}^{-1/4}

where GrH=RaHPrGr_{H}=\frac{Ra_{H}}{Pr}

and, we have δνδT∼Pr1/2\frac{\delta_{\nu}}{\delta_{T}} \sim Pr^{1/2}

Non-dimensionalising Governing Equations

Non-dimensional parameters x*=xH,y*=yH x^{*} = \frac{x}{H}, y^{*}=\frac{y}{H}

u*=uUref,v*=vUref u^{*} = \frac{u}{U_{ref}}, v^{*} = \frac{v}{U_{ref}}

θ=T−T∞Tw−T∞ \theta = \frac{T-T_{\infty}}{T_{w}-T_{\infty}}

Continuity

∂u*∂x*+∂v*∂y*=0 \frac{ \partial u^{*} }{ \partial x^{*} } + \frac{ \partial v^{*} }{ \partial y^{*} }=0

u*∂v*∂x*+v*∂v*∂y*=1ReH∂2v*∂x*2+gβ(Tw−T∞)HUref2θ u^{*} \frac{ \partial v^{*} }{ \partial x^{*} } + v^{*} \frac{ \partial v^{*} }{ \partial y^{*} } = \frac{1}{\mathrm{Re}_{H}} \frac{ \partial^{2} v^{*} }{ \partial x^{*2} } + \frac{g\beta(T_{w}-T_{\infty})H}{U_{ref}^{2}} \theta

Energy equation

u*∂θ∂x*+v*∂θ∂y*=1ReHPr∂2θ∂x*2 u^{*} \frac{ \partial \theta }{ \partial x^{*} } + v^{*} \frac{ \partial \theta }{ \partial y^{*} } = \frac{1}{\mathrm{Re}_{H}Pr} \frac{ \partial^{2} \theta }{ \partial x^{*2} }

We define Grashof number, GrH=gβ(Tw−T∞)HUref2θGr_{H}=\frac{g\beta(T_{w}-T_{\infty})H}{U_{ref}^{2}}\theta and Richardson number, RiH=GrHReH2Ri_{H}=\frac{Gr_{H}}{\mathrm{Re}_{H}^{2}}

Richardson Number, RiHRi_{H}

RiH=GrHReH2 Ri_{H}=\frac{Gr_{H}}{\mathrm{Re}_{H}^{2}}

Criteria Type of convection
Ri≫1Ri \gg 1 Natural
